# Agile Ceremonies & Team Collaboration

## Research Question

**What are the main Agile ceremonies, and what is their purpose?**

- Daily Stand-ups: For each member to share what they have done since the last meeting, what are being working on and if there are any issue. This keep the team up to date about the progress of the project.
- Sprint Planning (Scrum): Plan out iteration of the project called sprint. Define and delegate task.
- Continuous Prioritization (Kanban): Backlog is continuously prioritized, teams pull task when they ready to work on them.
- Retrospectives: Review the process, usually at the end of sprint, find out what went well and what not to improve.

**How do Agile teams collaborate asynchronously and across time zones?**
Team member and between different teams using written update instead of live meeting, using reports, task comments or messages. Everything should be well documented, so that the knowledge and necessary information about the project could be easily access. If meeting is necessary, a good schedule could be made, using a small overlap window where everyone online to host meeting and discuss important ideas.

## Reflection

**How do Agile ceremonies help with communication and alignment?**
Agile ceremonies such as daily-standup could allow team member regularly update to their team about the progress of the task, share any issue if needed, and the whole team know how far the project has gone, align everyone with the current pace. Furthermore, sprint planning allow all the team to know the goal and requirement of the project, make sure everyone have the same goal for the project

**Which ceremony do you think is most important for your role, and why?**
Daily-standup is the most important, as this allow me to share my progress very frequently, and if I have any issue, I could look for help early. Furthermore, when working in cyber security roles, if there are any security issue, I could address it early with the team, which is very important.
