# Data Privacy & Confidentiality

## Research Question

**Review Focus Bear privacy policy and note key takeaways**

- Focus Bear collect and process personal data (mostly provided by the user), which all considered confidential
- Under GDPR, Focus Bear have to protect and process the data fairly and lawfully
- According to GDPR, user have rights to access, delete and manage their data and consent

**What types of data are considered confidential at Focus Bear?**

- Any personal data are considered confidential

**What are best practices for handling confidential data?**

- Not sharing those data with anyone outside the organization
- Only giving necessary data to third-party services when needed, and have to be a trustworthy third-party
- Handling the data carefully, implement best security practice to protect confidential data whenever possible

**How should you respond to a suspected data breach or accidental disclosure of confidential information?**

- Immediately try to contain the breach, no letting anymore data being breach, look for the service that disclose the information and close it as soon as possible.
- Within the organization, the incident need to be reported immediately (to IT security team, manager, etc.): what happen, when it happen, what data being breached.
- Follow any legal requirement where the data is breach: report to authority, assessing the breach.

## Reflection

**What steps can you take to ensure you handle data securely in your daily tasks?**

- Avoid sharing any personal information to any services if not strictly necessary
- Protect your data, put them in secure location, if on cloud, make sure to follow best security practice such as using good password.
- Delete any unused or unnecessary data that could potentially be sensitive.

**How should you store, share, and dispose of sensitive information safely?**

- Avoid sharing and disposing sensitive if possible, if you have to, only giving those information to well-known and trusted services.
- Sensitive should usually be stored near you, or at place that you and only you could access. If you are using cloud storage, make sure secure your account with good password and MFA.

**What are some common mistakes that lead to data privacy issues, and how can they be avoided?**

- Sharing too much information online, especially sensitive one: Only share these information when strictly needed.
- Implement bad security practice leading to data being easily access: Using strong password, adding MFA
- Not deleting old and unused data that could be sensitive: Try not storing too much old but sensitive data that no longer needed
