# Inclusive Design Reflection

## Research Question

**Who are considered vulnerable populations, and what challenges might they face in digital spaces?**
Elderly people, people with physical or mental disability, indigenous people or people from rural area, children.

Vulnerable populations facing challenges such as they have less chance to access the digital space, difficult to afford devices and service necessary for digital world. Their online safety are also an issue as this population could be the target for cyber bully and online scam. Many service and website do not design with fully accessibility features, therefore could be difficult for certain people to navigate and use the service.

**What ethical considerations are important when designing or working with neurodivergent individuals?**
Avoid building too sophisticate and overwhelming UX, simple and straightforward step in using the service should be prioritized.

Minimize sensory features like harsh flashing light or strong light, distracting sound and noise, avoid complex visual pattern.

Communication should direct, only using plain and explicit language. Avoid sacarsm, metaphors or idioms, those that could cause confusion in communicating.

Be predictable, any meeting or changes in schedule/routine should be notice in advance, reduce any unnecessary axiety.

**How can you make interactions and content more accessible for people with ADHD or Autism?**

Buiding a simple and straighforward navigation system, reduce the amount of sub-page or sub-tab in the navigation system.

Using simple text and language to convey ideas in the app or website, reduce confusion and distraction.

Simple color pattern for the design.

**How can we support neurodivergent team members in a professional setting?**

Try to be clear and precise when communicate with the team members, avoid confusion.

Respect different person working styles, allow them to work in their most productive environment.

Allow some flexibility, and be patient with everyone.

## Reflection
