# Professionalism Reflection

## Research Question

**What are examples of professional vs. unprofessional behaviour in a casual work environment?**

- Professional: Talking politely, respectfully. Punctual, always attend meeting and giving notice if there are issues. Supportive, collaborate with the team, exchange ideas frequently.
- Unprofessional: Talking rude, harsh. Being late or not showing up at meeting, not giving any notice about their situation. Doing everything by themselve, judging without constructive feedback, not communicate with the team.

**What does respectful communication look like in remote teams?**
Message being clear, direct but nice, no personal attack or harsh tone.

When there are disagreements, everyone not trying to win the argument. Instead, each side list out what causing the disagreement and why they disagree and what they propose for the different approach. A meeting is necessary if the conflict could not be resolve over exchange message or email, here everyone could express their ideas and issue more clearly.

**How can you give and receive feedback professionally?**

Person giving feedback can be direct, tell them what is the issue, but should do it in a nice and constructive way, no personal attack in the feedback. If possible, provide suggestion on how they could do better. Focus the feedback on the action, not on the person themselve.

For the one receive the feedback, receive them respectfully. Not looking at feedback as personal attack, but a suggestion from teammate on how to improve yourselve, how to resolve problems. Ask them for more clarification, what cause the issue instead of being defensively and avoid responsibility.

## Reflection

**Have you ever experienced or witnessed great teamwork and professionalism? What made it effective?**
I did, and the best one was the team where we need to do a project and communicate with a real client. What made it effective:

- We divided the work evenly among the team members but appropriate to everyone skills. We all trying to complete task before deadline early to check everyone work and catch up if needed.
- We have regular meeting, both online and in-person to discuss any issue. Among the team, everyone respect each other, keeping the atmosphere fun and relax.
- With our client, we have a good relationship, we communication clearly and early with our client for any issue, being as professional as possible.

**What steps can you take to make sure your communication is clear, respectful, and inclusive?**

- Write and send message with clear content, avoid personal attack or anything to could be misunderstand.
- Being on schedule, if something changes, giving early notice.
- Being nice, patient to everyone. Keep a good attitute in messages and meetings.

**How can you help create a positive, fun, and professional work environment?**
Communicate with teammate or any person at work with a positive and respectful attitute, smiling and a little joke would help a lot. If there are any issue, be very clear and honest about, this would avoid any confusion among the team.

**How can you prepare effectively for meetings with supervisors and colleagues?**
Know what the meeting is about, and know what you need to talk about in the meeting. Plan out each of the thing and topic you need to talk about, if there are issue, include that in the plan, a small practice could really help. Write it in some note would help you not forget what you need to communicate with your colleagues.

**What would it look like to be proactive during your internship?**
Attend meeting regularly to share your progress. If there are any issue, try to communicate with teammate or supervisor as soon as possible instead of keeping it to yourself. Contribute ideas and discuss with your team whenever possible.

**How might you follow up with colleagues or supervisors if you need information/action from them? How would you escalate it further if you don't get a response and when would you escalate?**
Contact them as soon as possible through their prefer methods, provide what you are missing and need to follow up.If need to escalate, I could try to contact other people that could possible reach the person I need. Depend on the emergency of the issue, change the method of contact to SMS or even call them if really need to. The escalate time will also depend on the emergency, a casual issue could wait for a week before escalation, but something critical may need to be escalate after one day.

**What are some things you won't do?**

- Being disrepectful to my supervisor and colleages
- Giving false promises
- Try to do everything by myself - if the issue really out of my ability, I would find help from my colleage.
